# Synthesize Research Outputs

You are creating synthesis documents that combine multiple research outputs into coherent narratives.

## Task

1. Ask user for synthesis topic or let them specify output files to combine
2. Read all relevant output files from `outputs/`
3. Read relevant context from `context/`
4. Create a comprehensive synthesis document that:
   - Integrates findings from multiple outputs
   - Resolves contradictions or tensions in the research
   - Builds a coherent narrative
   - Provides evidence-based conclusions
   - Identifies remaining gaps or uncertainties
5. Save to `synthesis/` directory with format: `YYYY-MM-DD-synthesis-topic.md`

## Synthesis Structure

- **Overview**: What this synthesis covers and why
- **Methodology**: Which outputs were synthesized and how
- **Key Findings**: Main takeaways organized logically
- **Cross-Jurisdictional Insights**: Patterns across different regions
- **Evidence Quality**: Assessment of source reliability
- **Gaps and Limitations**: What we still don't know
- **Implications**: What this means for policy
- **Recommendations**: Evidence-based suggestions
- **Sources**: All outputs and context files referenced

## Guidelines

- Distinguish between well-established facts and preliminary findings
- Note where outputs agree vs. diverge
- Prioritize recent and jurisdiction-specific data
- Flag areas needing additional research
- Make synthesis suitable as foundation for lobby content
